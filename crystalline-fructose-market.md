<p><p>The crystalline fructose market comprises two primary segments: starch hydrolysis and sucrose hydrolysis. Starch hydrolysis involves the enzymatic breakdown of starch into glucose, which is then converted into fructose, providing a high-fructose syrup alternative. Conversely, sucrose hydrolysis involves the enzymatic splitting of sucrose into glucose and fructose, yielding pure crystalline fructose. Both methods serve diverse applications in food and beverages, catering to varying consumer preferences for sweetness and texture while also addressing dietary and health trends.</p></p>
