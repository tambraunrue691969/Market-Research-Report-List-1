<p><p>The drainage stent market primarily comprises two types: metal and plastic stents. Metal stents, often made of materials like nickel-titanium alloys, provide greater durability and are used in long-term applications. They are generally preferred for patients requiring extended support due to conditions like strictures. In contrast, plastic stents are usually more cost-effective and are often used for temporary drainage solutions. These can be easier to manipulate and remove, making them suitable for short-term treatments in various medical procedures.</p></p>
