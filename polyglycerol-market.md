<p><p>The polyglycerol market is segmented based on the degree of polymerization, leading to various types including PG2, PG3, PG4, PG6, and PG10. PG2 consists of two glycerol units and is often used in emulsification. PG3, with three units, is utilized for its surfactant properties. PG4 serves as a thickening agent, while PG6 and PG10, containing six and ten units respectively, find applications in food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals due to their stability and ability to enhance texture. Other types may include variations for specialized uses.</p></p>
