<p><p>Biomedical textiles encompass various types including non-woven, woven, and knitted textiles. Non-woven textiles are produced by bonding fibers, ideal for applications like surgical gowns and drapes due to their barrier properties. Woven textiles, created by interlacing yarns, offer strength and durability, suitable for implants and prosthetics. Knitted textiles, characterized by their flexibility and comfort, are often used in soft tissue repair and medical garments. Each type serves unique functions in the biomedical field, enhancing healthcare applications.</p></p>
