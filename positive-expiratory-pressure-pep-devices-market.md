High pressure PEP devices operate within a range of 26-102 cm H2O, suitable for patients requiring significant airway clearance, often in acute or chronic respiratory conditions. In contrast, low pressure PEP devices, operating at 5-20 cm H2O, are designed for general respiratory therapy, promoting airflow and mucus clearance in less severe cases. Both types aim to enhance lung function and improve respiratory health.</p></p>
