<p><p>The laboratory plasticware market is diverse, encompassing various materials such as HDPE (high-density polyethylene), known for its strength and chemical resistance; LDPE (low-density polyethylene), valued for flexibility; and PP (polypropylene), which is durable and heat-resistant. PPCO (polypropylene with a copolymer) provides added impact resistance. PMP (polymethylpentene) offers lightweight and clarity, while PVC (polyvinyl chloride) is widely used for its versatility. PETG (glycol-modified polyethylene terephthalate) combines clarity with strength, while PC (polycarbonate) is known for its high impact resistance. PS (polystyrene) is economical and easy to mold, and FEP/PFA (fluoropolymers) provides excellent chemical resistance, suitable for specialized applications.</p></p>
