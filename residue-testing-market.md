<p><p>Residue testing methods are essential for ensuring safety and compliance in various industries. Chromatography separates compounds for analysis of residues, while mass spectrometry identifies and quantifies these compounds with high accuracy. ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) detects specific residues using antibodies, making it effective for biological samples. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) amplifies DNA to identify genetic material from contaminants. Other methods may include immunoassays and biosensors, offering diverse options for residue detection across food safety, environmental monitoring, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
