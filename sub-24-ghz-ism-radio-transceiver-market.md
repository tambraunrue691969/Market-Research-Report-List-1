<p><p>The Sub-2.4 GHz ISM radio transceiver market features various modulation types for efficient communication. FSK (Frequency Shift Keying) and GFSK (Gaussian Frequency Shift Keying) enhance signal robustness, while MSK (Minimum Shift Keying) offers spectral efficiency. OOK (On-Off Keying) and ASK (Amplitude Shift Keying) are simpler techniques for lower power applications. GMSK (Gaussian Minimum Shift Keying) combines advantages of FSK and spectral efficiency. FM (Frequency Modulation) ensures clear audio transmission, while LoRa provides long-range communication with low power, and PSK (Phase Shift Keying) improves data rate.</p></p>
