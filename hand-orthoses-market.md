<p><p>The hand orthoses market is divided into two main types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to accommodate functional movement, aiding in rehabilitation by allowing controlled motion while providing support. They are often used for conditions requiring active therapy. In contrast, static orthotics offer rigid fixation, immobilizing the hand and wrist to promote healing during injuries or post-surgery. Each type serves distinct therapeutic purposes, catering to varying patient needs in hand function and recovery.</p></p>
