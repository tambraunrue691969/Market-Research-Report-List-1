<p><p>Insect Growth Regulators (IGRs) are chemicals that disrupt the development and reproduction of insects. The market is categorized into three types: Chitin Synthesis Inhibitors, which prevent the formation of chitin, essential for exoskeleton development; Juvenile Hormone Analogs & Mimics, which interfere with normal hormonal processes, preventing maturation; and Anti-Juvenile Hormone Agents, which promote premature metamorphosis. Each type serves to control pest populations by targeting their growth and lifecycle stages, making them effective in agricultural and urban pest management.</p></p>
