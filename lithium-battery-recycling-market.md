<p><p>The lithium battery recycling market comprises several types based on battery chemistry. LiCoO2 batteries, primarily used in consumer electronics, are known for high energy density but contain cobalt, raising ethical mining concerns. NMC batteries combine nickel, manganese, and cobalt, balancing performance and cost; they're common in electric vehicles. LiFePO4 batteries, favored for their thermal stability and safety, are prevalent in energy storage systems. The "Other" category encompasses emerging battery technologies and chemistries, reflecting ongoing innovation in recycling methodologies.</p></p>
