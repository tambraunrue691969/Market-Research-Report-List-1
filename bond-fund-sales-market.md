<p><p>Bond fund sales encompass various market types, including mutual funds, closed-end funds, unit investment trusts (UITs), and exchange-traded funds (ETFs). Mutual funds pool investor money to purchase bonds, offering diversification and professional management. Closed-end funds issue a fixed number of shares, trading on exchanges at varying prices. UITs provide a fixed portfolio of bonds for a specific duration. ETFs combine the features of mutual funds and stocks, allowing for intraday trading and lower expense ratios, enhancing accessibility for investors.</p></p>
